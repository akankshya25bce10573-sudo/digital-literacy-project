
Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist (For College Students in India)

General Online Safety
	•	Use Strong Passwords
Create unique passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols for each account.
	•	Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
Add an extra layer of security to email, social media, and banking apps.
	•	Verify Before You Click
Avoid clicking on suspicious links in emails, messages, or social media.
	•	Keep Software Updated
Regularly update your phone, apps, and antivirus to fix security vulnerabilities.
	•	Limit Personal Information Sharing
Do not share sensitive details like address, college ID, or travel plans publicly.
	•	Use Secure Wi-Fi Networks
Avoid logging into important accounts on public Wi-Fi networks.


UPI & Financial Safety (Important)
	•	Never Share OTP or UPI PIN
Banks or apps will never ask for your PIN or OTP.
	•	Verify Payment Requests Carefully
Always check the receiver’s name before approving any UPI request.
	•	Avoid Unknown QR Codes
Scanning random QR codes can lead to unauthorized payments.


Awareness & Response
	•	Be Alert to Urgency or Threat Messages
Scammers often create panic (e.g., “Account will be blocked”) to trick you.
	•	Log Out from Shared Devices
Always log out after using cyber cafés or shared systems.
	•	Report Suspicious Activity Immediately
Do not ignore unusual account activity or fraud attempts.

 Reporting Cybercrime (India)
	•	National Cyber Crime Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in
	•	Helpline Number: 1930
